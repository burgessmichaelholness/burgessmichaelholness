Dreams Across the Ocean: the Jamaican immigrant story readers are calling "a love letter to every family who ever left home to build a future."

When Malik boards the plane in Montego Bay, he carries more than luggage he carries the weight of his family's hopes, the memory of his grandmother's stories, and a dream that stretches 4,500 miles across the Atlantic.

Set against the backdrop of 1980s London, this powerful debut novel follows one man's journey from the warm Caribbean sun to the cold reality of building a new life in a foreign land. Through Malik's eyes, we experience the struggle between holding onto home and embracing the unknown, the pain of separation, and the triumph of the human spirit.

Key Themes That Resonate

At the heart of Dreams Across the Ocean are universal truths that speak to every reader, regardless of their journey.

Resilience

Malik's journey embodies the unbreakable spirit of those who dare to dream beyond their circumstances. Every setback becomes a setup for a comeback.

Identity

In the space between two cultures, Malik discovers who he truly is not defined by where he comes from, but by who he chooses to become.

Hope

Across every ocean and through every storm, hope remains the compass that guides Malik home not to a place, but to a purpose.

From Jamaican Roots to Global Vision

Burgess Michael Holness was born in Montego Bay, Jamaica, where the Caribbean Sea whispered stories of adventure and possibility. His own journey across the ocean to the United Kingdom in the early 2000s inspired the narrative that would become Dreams Across the Ocean.

Drawing from the rich oral tradition of his grandmother's storytelling and his experiences as part of the Jamaican diaspora, Burgess crafts fiction that bridges cultures and generations. His writing explores themes of identity, belonging, and the universal human desire to create a better future for those we love.

"Every immigrant carries a library of stories in their heart. I write to give those stories a voice, to honor the sacrifices made before us, and to remind the world that dreams know no borders."

When not writing, Burgess advocates for literacy programs in underserved communities and mentors aspiring authors from Caribbean backgrounds. He believes in the power of representation and the responsibility of storytellers to reflect the full spectrum of human experience.
